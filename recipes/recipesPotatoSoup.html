What to use

  • 4-5 medium red or gold potatoes, peeled and cut into large chunks (I usually cut each into about 32 pieces)
  • 3/4 cup dry quick barley
  • 8 cups water
  • 8 tsp chicken bouilon or 12 tsp beef bouilon (I prefer beef)
  • 1 carrot, peeled and cut into small chunks approximately 1 inch thick
  • 3-4 stalks of celery, cut into small chunks approximately 1 inch thick
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced or crushed
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 tbsp freshly chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste (I used about 4 tsps)

How to use it

  1. In a large pot, bring water and bouilon to a boil
  2. Add chopped potatoes to the pot. If using standard barley instead of quick barley, add the barley in about 5 minutes before adding the potatoes
  3. While potatoes boil, heat a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the celery and carrots for about 3-4 minutes
  4. Add the garlic, Italian seasoning, and parsley to the celery and carrots then cook for another 1 minute
  5. Add the barley and everything in the skillet to the pot with the broth
  6. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 12-15 minutes, until barley has softened
  7. Add salt and pepper to taste
